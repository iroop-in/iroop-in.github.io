E-Learning

E-learning can be described as a learning system comprised of structured/unstructured teaching made possible with the help of online and electronic resources. While teaching can be based in or out of the classrooms, the use of computers and the Internet forms the major component of E-learning. E-learning can also be termed as a network enabled transfer of skills and knowledge, and the delivery of education is made to a large number of recipients at the same or different times. Earlier, it was not accepted wholeheartedly as it was assumed that this system lacked the human element required in learning.

Customer Disengagement

Recently, plenty of research was conducted on engagement more particular on enhancing Customer Engagement (CE). The flip side of CE, namely Customer Disengagement (CDE), seems to be uncharted. Thus,this report elaborates a working definition on CDE. This includes the interrelation of CE and CDE. According to this report, CDE is described as a psychological process that can cause the state of complete detachment from a Brand or Service (B/S). CDE could have behavioural outcomes in terms of Negative Customer Engagement (NCE). Additionally, it interrelates with former CE levels and varies across contexts. CDE is supposed to be a promising research field.

What is Gamification?

Gamification is the process of taking something that already exists – a website, an enterprise application, an online community – and integrating game mechanics into it to motivate participation, engagement, loyalty and to obtain game dynamics from it.
- Nir eyal

Gamification — the application of game mechanics into nongame activities and processes—is a proven, powerful strategy for engaging, influencing and motivating diverse groups of people. The business community has just begun to realize the power of gamification to enhance customer interaction, build loyalty, andincentivize employees and partners. And the concept has the potential to solve a variety of obstacles outside the traditional business environment as well, in areas such as Health & Wellness, Education & Training and Public Policy & Government.

Gamification as per recent researches has found its way in user engagement and retention. Consumers of e-Learning platforms tend to get bored with one way communication, followed by poor retention of users in this competitive world. Thus studying user behavior to get things done has become a popular among platforms. Game elements helps to engage user by giving sense of achievement, loss of progress or competitiveness among peers. Major giants like Facebook and Instagram thrive on gamification for maintaining the huge user base.

Problem

In starting phase of a product based startup, the most delicate concern surmounts the lead generation. Though decent userbase exist for the company (20000+ registrations of professional chess players as well as aspirants) the stats clearly projects retention of users as the major issue for the time being. This indicated that although the organization exhibit expansive growth in registrations, it still lacked a good retention of students on our e-learning platform. The log analysis report (section 3.1) put forward the dropout rates across existing content on platform, thus depicting the lack of favourable consumer interaction elements on the platform.

Following points jot down the major issues involved:

  1. Lack of data point in log analysis to measure existing engaging components on company’s website.

  2. Non-existence of feedback forms to capture direct experiences of the students engaging on platform: The sales team in collaboration of world class GMs was responsible for introducing and materializing the products, but still lacked the user feedbacks (qualitative as well as quantitative) capturing consumer experience on the newly launched platform

  3. Absence of tracking behavioural aspects of users engaging on platform followed by lack of user profiling for personalization: User dynamics play a vital role in retention. Capturing and analysing user interaction data (clicks, scrolls, hovers) across various product categories (dream room, camp room, live room) becomes crucial on what times across global locations

  4. Lack of behavioural studies to employ great engaging elements that works in today’s world.
